Floating Girl

A sketch illustration

The illustration was created for a poem. The poem talked about how sometimes a person fell down and worthless. But once they Icom their lessons and try they rise again in all the odds that surround them

The Sketch was created by a friend Ms. kritika Shrivastava which is transformed from sheet to artboard.

Illustration has two versions; one is a bleak, grey-white-black version and the other one is relatively colorful. The drawing depicts a person surrounded, essentially encapsulated by some structures. In the bleaker version of the drawing, if we talk about symbolism, we can think of the structures depicting problems and how they are at focus. In the second version (the color-laden one), the same structures still encapsulate the person, but it looks less overwhelming, most likely because of the use of color. The use of color (as in how the color has been changed from grey to more vibrant shades) can be a depiction of "perspective" and how change in perspective and actions can yield different results; in this case, the picture with color looks more appealing than it's bleak counterpart.
